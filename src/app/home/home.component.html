1. Essential Supplies: Donate non-perishable food, bottled water, and hygiene items to meet immediate needs

2. Clothing and Bedding: Provide clean, dry clothing and bedding to offer comfort and warmth

3. Cleaning Supplies: Offer cleaning tools and materials to help with post-flood cleanup efforts

Why donating money?

1. Effective Aid: Monetary donations allow organizations to allocate resources efficiently based on the specific needs of each situation

2. Flexibility: Money donations provide flexibility to respond quickly to emergencies and adapt to changing circumstances

3. Cost-Efficient: Sending money directly to organizations reduces administrative costs and ensures more funds go directly to those in need


What to donate for victims of earthquakes?

1. Emergency Supplies: Donate essential items such as bottled water, non-perishable food and blankets to meet immediate needs

2. Shelter and Clothing: Provide tents, sleeping bags, and clean clothing to offer temporary shelter and comfort

3. Medical Supplies: Medical equipment, first-aid supplies, and medicines to support the injured and medical teams in providing essential care during the aftermath of the earthquake


How to determine if something is worth donating?

1. Condition: Check if the item is in good condition for donation

2. Relevance: Ensure the donation matches the recipients' needs or the organization's guidelines

3. Impact: Consider the potential positive impact the donation could have

4. Ask yourself - would you like to have it or would you like your kid to have it?

If the answer is "yes", then you're good to go - Make a donation
